Introduction

Sea level rise is a critical global issue, mainly caused by thermal expansion and the melting of ice. It impacts coastal regions worldwide and is a major consequence of climate change.

Processes Leading to Sea Level Rise

Thermal Expansion

As the global temperature rises, ocean water expands, contributing to rising sea levels. This process is called thermal expansion.

Melting Ice Sheets

As the Earth's temperature increases, glaciers and ice caps melt, releasing freshwater into the oceans and raising sea levels.

Glacial Isostatic Adjustment

When ice sheets melt, the Earth's crust can rebound, affecting local sea levels.

Impact of Sea Level Rise

Sea level rise affects coastal cities, ecosystems, and human populations. Inundation of coastal areas, saltwater intrusion, and extreme weather events are some of the consequences.

Solutions to Mitigate Sea Level Rise

Mitigating sea level rise involves reducing greenhouse gas emissions, protecting coastal ecosystems, and building resilient infrastructure.
